Why Dissertations Require Interview Consent Forms

Dissertation research involving human subjects — including qualitative interviews — requires ethical approval and informed consent from all participants. Whether you are conducting a PhD dissertation, master's thesis, or undergraduate honors project, your institution's IRB or Research Ethics Committee (REC) must approve your consent forms before data collection begins.

Dissertation interview consent forms serve two purposes: they protect participant rights and they demonstrate to your examining committee that you conducted ethically sound research. Many dissertations are rejected or returned for revisions due to inadequate consent documentation.

Timeline Tip: Submit your IRB application with your consent form at least 4-6 weeks before your planned interview start date. IRB review takes time, and many students underestimate this when planning dissertation timelines.

What to Include in a Dissertation Consent Form

  • Your name, degree program, department, and university
  • Your dissertation supervisor's name and contact information
  • IRB or ethics committee protocol number and approval date
  • Clear, plain-language description of your dissertation topic and research purpose
  • Interview format, duration, and topics to be covered
  • Whether and how the interview will be recorded
  • Confidentiality provisions and anonymization approach
  • Right to withdraw and deadline for data withdrawal requests
  • How findings will be published or used (e.g., academic thesis accessible via university library)
  • Participant and researcher signature block

Dissertation Interview Consent Form Example

DISSERTATION INTERVIEW CONSENT FORM Researcher (Doctoral Candidate): [Your Full Name] Degree Program: [PhD/MA/MSc in Field], [University Name] Dissertation Title: [Full Dissertation Title or Working Title] Supervisor: [Supervisor's Name], [Department] Contact Email: [Your Email] Phone: [Your Phone] IRB/Ethics Approval #: [Reference Number] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ INTRODUCTION I am a [PhD/Master's] student at [University] currently completing my dissertation on [brief description]. I am seeking participants for interviews as part of my primary data collection. PURPOSE OF THE RESEARCH This study examines [topic] with the aim of [research objectives]. The findings will form part of my [PhD/Master's] dissertation and may be published in academic journals or conference proceedings. INTERVIEW DETAILS If you agree to participate, you will be asked to take part in a [semi-structured / in-depth] interview lasting approximately [X] minutes. The interview will be conducted [in person / via Zoom / by phone]. You will be asked questions about [topic areas]. RECORDING With your consent, the interview will be audio-recorded for transcription purposes only. Recordings will not be shared and will be deleted after transcription is complete. ☐ I consent to audio recording. ☐ I do not consent to recording (notes only). CONFIDENTIALITY All identifying information will be removed from transcripts and any published findings. You will be assigned a pseudonym. Only I and my supervisor will have access to the raw data. DISSERTATION ACCESS My completed dissertation will be deposited in [University Library Name] and may be available online through institutional repositories. Your anonymized responses may be quoted in the dissertation. VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION AND WITHDRAWAL Your participation is voluntary. You may withdraw from the study and request deletion of your data at any time up to [date] without penalty. QUESTIONS Please contact me at [email] with any questions. You may also contact my supervisor, [Name], at [email]. ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ PARTICIPANT CONSENT I have read and understood the above information and agree to participate. Participant Name (Print): ____________________________________ Participant Signature: _______________________________________ Date: ______________________________________________________ Researcher Signature: _______________________________________ Date: ______________________________________________________

Thesis Interview Consent Form

Master's thesis interview consent forms follow the same structure as PhD dissertation forms but may have a slightly simpler IRB process (expedited rather than full review in many cases). The key elements — purpose, procedures, confidentiality, voluntary participation, and signature block — remain the same.

University Requirements

Pepperdine University

Pepperdine's IRB requires all dissertation interview consent forms to follow their template, available through the university's research compliance portal. Doctoral students must obtain IRB approval before any data collection.

SNHU (Southern New Hampshire University)

SNHU's IRB reviews all student research involving human subjects. Interview consent forms must meet APA ethical standards. SNHU students can download the required consent form template from the IRB office.

UTS (University of Technology Sydney)

UTS requires all research involving human participants to be approved by the Human Research Ethics Committee (HREC). Consent forms must comply with the Australian National Statement on Ethical Conduct in Human Research.

UK Universities

UK universities (UCL, University of Edinburgh, etc.) require consent forms to comply with GDPR and the UK Research Integrity Office guidelines. Forms must include a data processing statement and reference to the Data Protection Act 2018.

What Is a Media Interview Consent Form and When Is It Required?

A media interview consent form — also called a media release form — is a legal document that grants a media organisation the right to record, broadcast, publish, and distribute an individual's appearance, voice, and statements in connection with a specific programme or production. Unlike a standard interview consent form, a media form covers the full chain of distribution: television broadcast, online streaming, theatrical release, social media syndication, and archived reruns.

Media consent forms are used across all professional media production formats. Television news programmes require them before broadcast. Documentary filmmakers need them to secure clearance for interview subjects that appear in the final cut. Online video creators and digital media publishers need them before embedding or publishing identifiable footage. For street interviews where consent is obtained immediately after filming in public, a simple one-page consent form is typically sufficient.

Media consent differs in scope from narrower consent forms used in other fields. A journalism interview consent form focuses on source attribution, on/off-record status, and quote use in print or digital articles. A podcast interview consent form covers audio syndication and clip rights. The media form presented here covers all of these plus the additional rights required for broadcast television, theatrical distribution, and film festivals.

For productions involving audio or video recording, the consent form must be signed before recording begins to be legally valid in two-party consent jurisdictions. Productions that include interviews with students or minors require a parent or guardian signature — see our student interview consent form for the appropriate template. For guidance on drafting consent forms from scratch, see our step-by-step guide on how to write an interview consent form, or browse our interview consent form samples and examples.

What to Include in a Media Interview Consent Form

  • ✓
    Media organisation, production company, or outlet nameIdentify the legal entity responsible for the production — the broadcaster, production company, or individual filmmaker.
  • ✓
    Journalist, producer, or director nameThe name of the person conducting the interview and their role in the production.
  • ✓
    Purpose of the interview — news, documentary, research, or entertainmentState the programme title, production format, and intended distribution channel so the subject knows exactly how their interview will be used.
  • ✓
    Recording consent — audio, video, and photographySpecify whether the subject will be audio recorded, video recorded, photographed, or all three. Required before recording in two-party consent jurisdictions.
  • ✓
    Broadcast and publication rights — all channelsList every distribution channel: television, online streaming, cinema, social media, podcast, and print. Failure to list a channel means you technically lack permission to distribute there. For audio-only use, also consider a podcast interview consent form.
  • ✓
    Geographic scope and duration of rightsSpecify whether rights are worldwide or territory-restricted, and whether they are granted in perpetuity or for a limited period. "Worldwide, in perpetuity" is standard for most broadcast and online productions.
  • ✓
    Editing consentConfirm that the production may edit, condense, or combine the interview with other material. For journalistic interviews, this may also include quote review rights.
  • ✓
    Attribution and identification in the mediaState whether the subject's name, job title, and/or likeness may be used to identify them in the programme, promotional materials, or press releases.
  • ✓
    Compensation or appearance feeDocument whether the subject is being paid an appearance fee, or whether they are appearing voluntarily without compensation.
  • ✓
    Signature lines for subject and producerBoth parties must sign and date the form. For street interviews, follow up with an email-based consent form before publishing if a signature cannot be obtained on-site.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a media interview consent form?+
A media interview consent form grants a media organisation the right to record, broadcast, and publish an interview subject's appearance and words across specified platforms and territories. It protects both the producer and the subject by documenting exactly what was agreed before filming begins. It differs from a journalism interview consent form in that it typically covers broader distribution rights including television broadcast, streaming, theatrical release, and syndication.
Do I need consent for street interviews?+
In most jurisdictions, yes. While you may not need consent simply to film in a public place, publishing identifiable footage of individuals in a broadcast or online video generally requires a signed media release form. A street interview consent form should be signed before you publish. For street interviews, a simple one-page consent form is typically practical to obtain on-site.
What is the difference between a media release form and an interview consent form?+
A media release form grants broad publication rights to audio, video, and images. A media interview consent form is more specific to the interview context and may include confidentiality provisions, withdrawal rights, and information about the specific programme. For research interviews within a documentary, a separate research interview consent form is often required. For audio-only content, a podcast interview consent form may be more appropriate.
Can I interview an incarcerated person?+
Interviewing incarcerated individuals requires additional consent considerations beyond a standard media interview consent form. You typically need approval from the correctional facility's administration in addition to the individual's own signed consent. Contact the facility's public affairs office to understand their media access policy before arranging the interview.
Does a media consent form give me copyright over the interview?+
A consent or release form grants you the right to use the interview content across specified platforms but does not automatically transfer copyright in the subject's underlying statements. For complex rights situations — especially for commercial broadcast or theatrical distribution — consult a media lawyer.

What to Include

  • Media organization, production company, or outlet name
  • Journalist, producer, or director name
  • Purpose of the interview (news, documentary, research, etc.)
  • Recording consent: audio, video, photo
  • Broadcast and publication rights: TV, online, social media, film
  • Right to edit for broadcast standards and time constraints
  • Attribution and identification in the media
  • Geographic and temporal scope of usage rights
  • Right to refuse specific questions
  • Contact details for the media organization
  • Compensation or release fee (if any)
  • Signature lines for subject and producer/journalist

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a media interview consent form? ▼
A media interview consent form grants a media organization the right to record, broadcast, and publish an interview subject's appearance and words across specified platforms and territories.
Do I need consent for street interviews? ▼
In most jurisdictions, you do need consent before broadcasting or publishing street interviews. While you may not need consent simply to film in a public place, publishing identifiable footage of individuals generally requires a signed release.
What is the difference between a media release form and an interview consent form? ▼
A media release form typically grants broad publication rights to audio, video, and images. An interview consent form is more specific to the interview context and may include more detail about confidentiality, off-record protections, and withdrawal rights.
Can I interview an inmate or detained person? ▼
Interviewing incarcerated individuals requires additional consent considerations and institutional permissions. You typically need approval from the correctional facility in addition to the individual's signed consent.
Does a media consent form give me copyright over the interview? ▼
A consent/release form grants you the right to use the interview content but does not automatically transfer copyright. The specific rights granted depend on the language of your form. Consult a media lawyer for complex rights situations.

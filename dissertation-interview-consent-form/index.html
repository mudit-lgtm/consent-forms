What to Include

  • Dissertation/thesis title and research degree level
  • Student researcher name, supervisor, and institution
  • University ethics approval reference number
  • Clear statement of dissertation research purpose
  • Interview format, duration, and number of sessions
  • Audio/video recording consent options
  • Transcription and anonymization procedures
  • Data storage and access (supervisor access disclosure)
  • Participant's right to withdraw without academic consequence
  • How findings will be used (dissertation, publications, conferences)
  • Supervisor contact information as second point of contact
  • Signature and date lines

DISSERTATION INTERVIEW CONSENT FORM

Dissertation Title: [Full Dissertation Title]
Student Researcher: [Full Name], [Degree Programme]
Supervisor: [Supervisor Name], [Department]
Institution: [University Name]
Ethics Approval Reference: [XXXXXXXX]
Student Email: [student@university.edu]
Supervisor Email: [supervisor@university.edu]

ABOUT THIS DISSERTATION RESEARCH
I am a [degree level] student at [University Name] conducting dissertation
research on [topic]. This study has received ethics approval from [ethics
committee name], reference [XXXXXXXX].

PURPOSE OF THE RESEARCH
This research aims to [describe dissertation research question and objectives].
Your participation will contribute to [describe significance].

THE INTERVIEW
If you agree to participate, I will conduct a [semi-structured / in-depth]
interview lasting approximately [duration]. The interview will take place
[in person / via [platform]]. I will ask questions about [topic areas].

RECORDING AND TRANSCRIPTION
With your permission, the interview will be audio recorded. The recording will
be transcribed and then deleted. Transcripts will be accessible only to myself
and my dissertation supervisor.

CONFIDENTIALITY AND ANONYMITY
[Choose: Your name will not be used in my dissertation. A pseudonym will be
assigned. / You will be identified only as described in the consent.]
Data will be stored securely and deleted after my dissertation is submitted
and examined.

VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION
Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time
without explanation. This will not affect our personal or professional
relationship in any way.

HOW FINDINGS WILL BE USED
Findings will be reported in my dissertation and may subsequently be used
in academic publications or conference presentations.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need ethics approval for my dissertation interviews? ▼
Most universities require undergraduate, master's, and PhD students to obtain ethics approval before conducting research interviews with human participants. Contact your department's ethics committee or research support office.
What information should I include about my dissertation supervisor? ▼
Include your supervisor's name and contact information as an alternative point of contact for participant queries. Note that your supervisor may have access to interview transcripts as part of their supervisory role.
What is a Pepperdine University dissertation consent form? ▼
Pepperdine University requires dissertation researchers to use their IRB-approved consent form template, which includes institution-specific language and contact information. Use this template as a starting point and customize it with Pepperdine's required elements.
Can I use email consent for my dissertation interviews? ▼
Some universities accept email consent as an alternative to signed forms for low-risk interview research. However, most prefer written consent. Check your institution's specific ethics requirements.
How long should I keep my dissertation consent forms? ▼
Typically, consent forms should be retained for the duration of the dissertation examination period plus a minimum of 1–3 years. Check your university's research data management policy for specific guidance.

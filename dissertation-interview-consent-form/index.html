Definition

What Is a Dissertation Interview Consent Form?

A dissertation interview consent form is a written document that a research participant signs before taking part in an interview conducted as part of a student's dissertation or thesis project. It formally records that the participant agrees to be interviewed, understands how their data will be used, and is aware of their right to withdraw at any time.

Unlike a general interview consent form, the dissertation version must include university-specific information: your ethics approval reference number, your supervisor's contact details, your degree level and programme, and a clear statement about how findings will appear in your submitted work. Most institutions require this form as part of their IRB or ethics committee application before any fieldwork can begin.

Whether you are conducting semi-structured qualitative interviews, focus groups, or oral history sessions, this consent form protects both you as the student researcher and your participants — giving you documented permission to use the material in your dissertation and any subsequent publications.

💡 University Tip: Most ethics committees will not approve your dissertation project without a completed consent form template attached to your application. Download this interview consent form template and adapt it before submitting your ethics application — not after.
Key Elements

What to Include in a Dissertation Interview Consent Form

A dissertation interview consent form must meet both your university's ethics requirements and the standards of a general informed interview consent form. Every element below should be present — ethics committees regularly reject applications for missing items.

  • ✓
    Dissertation Title and Degree Level
    Full dissertation title plus your degree programme (PhD, MSc, MA, BA) and university department.
  • ✓
    Student Researcher and Supervisor Details
    Your name, student ID or email, and your supervisor's name, department, and contact email as a second point of contact.
  • ✓
    Ethics Approval Reference Number
    The reference issued by your institution's IRB or ethics committee. Required at most universities before conducting any research interviews.
  • ✓
    Research Purpose and Interview Description
    A plain-language summary of what your dissertation is about and what the participant will be asked during the interview.
  • ✓
    Recording and Transcription Arrangements
    Whether the interview will be audio recorded, video recorded, or transcribed. State who has access and when recordings will be deleted. For online interviews, see our online interview consent form.
  • ✓
    Confidentiality and Anonymisation
    How participant identity will be protected — pseudonyms, anonymised transcripts, restricted access — and the data retention period after examination.
  • ✓
    Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal Rights
    Clear statement that participation is voluntary and the participant may withdraw at any time without academic, professional, or personal consequence.
  • ✓
    How Findings Will Be Used
    State that findings will appear in the dissertation and potentially in academic publications or conference presentations.
  • ✓
    Participant and Researcher Signature Lines
    Printed name, signature, and date fields for both the participant and the student researcher. For online interviews, email confirmation is accepted at many universities.
Step-by-Step Guide

How to Write a Dissertation Interview Consent Form

Follow these 8 steps to write a dissertation interview consent form that satisfies your university's ethics committee. For the full guide, see How to Write an Interview Consent Form.

  1. State the Dissertation Title and Degree Level

    Include the full dissertation title, your degree programme (PhD, MSc, BA), your name, and your university department at the top of the form. Use the exact dissertation title as registered with your institution.

  2. Add Ethics Approval Details

    Include your university ethics committee name and approval reference number. Most universities — including those using an IRB process — require this before any fieldwork begins. Contact your department's research support office if you have not yet applied.

  3. Describe the Research Purpose and Interview Format

    Explain what your dissertation research aims to achieve in plain, jargon-free language. State the interview format — whether it will be a semi-structured qualitative interview, a structured interview, or an oral history session — and the expected duration.

  4. Specify Recording and Transcription

    State clearly whether the interview will be audio recorded, video recorded, or transcribed. Specify who will access the recording (you and your supervisor only), and when recordings will be permanently deleted after submission. See also our recording interview consent form.

  5. Explain Confidentiality and Data Storage

    Describe how participant data will be anonymised — typically using pseudonyms — stored securely (encrypted storage, password-protected files), and deleted after the dissertation examination period. Disclose clearly if your supervisor will have access to transcripts as part of their supervisory role.

  6. Add the Voluntary Participation Statement

    Confirm that participation is entirely voluntary and that the participant may withdraw at any time without penalty. Crucially for dissertation research, state explicitly that withdrawal will not affect the participant's academic, professional, or personal relationship with you or your institution.

  7. Include Supervisor as Second Point of Contact

    Add your dissertation supervisor's full name, email address, and department. Participants must be able to raise concerns independently of the student researcher. This is a standard requirement at most UK and US universities for research interview consent forms.

  8. Add Signature and Date Lines

    Include printed name, signature, and date fields for both the participant and yourself as the researcher. For online and Zoom interviews, a written email reply confirming consent is accepted at many institutions as an alternative to a physical signature.

ABOUT THIS RESEARCH

I am a [degree level] student at [University Name] conducting dissertation
research on [topic area]. This study has received ethics approval from
[ethics committee name], reference [XXXXXXXX].

PURPOSE

This research aims to [describe your dissertation research question and
objectives in plain language]. Your participation will contribute to
[describe the significance of your research].

THE INTERVIEW

If you agree to participate, I will conduct a [semi-structured / in-depth /
structured] interview lasting approximately [duration, e.g. 45 minutes].
The interview will take place [in person at location / via Zoom / by phone].
I will ask you questions about [topic areas].

RECORDING AND TRANSCRIPTION

With your permission, the interview will be audio recorded. The recording
will be transcribed and then permanently deleted. Transcripts will be
accessible only to myself and my dissertation supervisor.

If you prefer not to be recorded, I will take notes only.

CONFIDENTIALITY AND ANONYMITY

Your name will not be used in my dissertation. You will be referred to
by a pseudonym throughout the write-up and in any subsequent publications.
All data will be stored in encrypted, password-protected files accessible
only to myself and my supervisor. Data will be deleted upon completion of
the examination process, typically within [timeframe] of submission.

VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time
and for any reason without needing to provide an explanation. Withdrawing
will have no effect on any academic, professional, or personal relationship.

HOW FINDINGS WILL BE USED

Results will be reported in my dissertation and may subsequently be used
in academic journal articles or conference presentations. Individual
participants will not be identifiable in any outputs.

CONSENT

I confirm that I have read and understood the information above. I have
had the opportunity to ask questions. I agree to participate in this
interview voluntarily.

[ ] I agree to the interview being audio recorded.
[ ] I agree to the interview being video recorded.
[ ] I do not consent to recording (notes only).

Participant Name (print): ________________________________

Participant Signature: ___________________________________ Date: __________

Researcher Signature:  ___________________________________ Date: __________

Thesis Research

Thesis Interview Consent Form

A thesis interview consent form serves the same purpose as a dissertation consent form, but the terminology "thesis" is more commonly used for undergraduate final-year projects and master's-level research in the UK, Australia, and Ireland, while "dissertation" typically refers to doctoral-level work in these regions. In the United States, "dissertation" usually refers to doctoral research and "thesis" to master's-level work.

Regardless of terminology, the elements of your consent form are identical: ethics approval reference, researcher and supervisor details, recording consent, confidentiality provisions, and voluntary participation statement. Download the sample above — it works equally well as a thesis interview consent form or dissertation consent form.

For student-level projects below dissertation or thesis level — including AP Research, undergraduate independent study, and school history projects — see our dedicated student interview consent form, which includes simplified language and parent consent provisions for interviewing minors.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a consent form for my dissertation interviews? +
Yes. Almost all universities require students to obtain signed interview consent forms before conducting research interviews with human participants — at undergraduate, master's, and PhD levels. This applies whether your dissertation uses qualitative interviews, semi-structured interviews, focus groups, or oral history methods. Most institutions will not pass your ethics application without a consent form meeting their IRB or ethics committee standards. See the BERA Ethical Guidelines and APA Ethics Code for field-specific requirements.
What is a sample informed consent form for a dissertation interview? +
A sample informed consent form for a dissertation interview is a pre-written example covering all required elements: dissertation title, researcher and supervisor details, ethics approval reference, research purpose, recording and transcription arrangements, confidentiality provisions, voluntary participation statement, and signature lines. You can download a free sample on this page, or browse our interview consent form samples and examples page.
What does a Pepperdine University dissertation consent form require? +
Pepperdine University's IRB requires dissertation researchers to use its approved informed consent form template, which must include the university's IRB contact details, the researcher's faculty supervisor information, CCPA or GDPR data rights language, and a statement about how findings will be used in publications. Download the template on this page and add Pepperdine's required boilerplate from their IRB portal.
Can I use the SNHU interview consent form template for my dissertation? +
Southern New Hampshire University (SNHU) requires students to use its own IRB-approved consent form template. The SNHU interview consent form PDF must include the student's SNHU ID, supervising faculty details, and SNHU-specific institutional language. Download the general template on this page and adapt it with SNHU's required elements, or access the official SNHU consent form directly from your SNHU student portal. The IRB interview consent form page has additional guidance on university-specific requirements.
How long should I retain dissertation interview consent forms? +
Most universities require dissertation consent forms to be kept for a minimum of 1 to 3 years after the dissertation is examined. Institutions following UK Research Integrity Office (UKRIO) guidelines may require 5 to 10 years for forms connected to publishable research. Always confirm the exact period with your institution's research data management policy before disposing of any consent records.
Can I use email consent for online dissertation interviews? +
Many universities accept email consent for low-risk online dissertation interviews. The participant must reply in writing confirming they have read the consent form and agree to participate. For Zoom or Skype interviews, emailing the consent form and receiving a written reply is widely accepted. Always verify your institution's specific policy. For guidance on remote consent processes, see our online interview consent form and recording interview consent form pages.
Does my supervisor need to be named on the dissertation consent form? +
Yes. Best practice — and the policy at most universities — is to include your dissertation supervisor's name, email, and department as a second point of contact. Participants must be able to raise concerns independently of the student researcher. If your supervisor will access recordings or transcripts, this must be clearly disclosed in the confidentiality section. For context on what the informed consent form for an interview requires, see our dedicated page.

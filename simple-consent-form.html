Simple Consent Form for Interview

A clean, one-page interview consent form with no legal jargon. Covers all the essentials: purpose, recording consent, confidentiality, and voluntary participation. Free download.

Why Use a Simple Consent Form?

Not every interview requires a multi-page IRB-style consent form. For many informal research contexts, student projects, journalistic interviews, oral history recordings, and simple podcasts, a short, plain-language consent form is perfectly appropriate and often more effective — participants are more likely to read and understand a simple form.

A simple consent form is appropriate when:

  • The interview poses minimal risk to participants
  • No formal IRB approval is required
  • The context is relatively informal (school project, podcast, oral history)
  • You need a quick, easy-to-complete form for in-person or phone use
  • Participants may be uncomfortable with lengthy legal-sounding documents

Simple One-Page Interview Consent Form

INTERVIEW CONSENT FORM Project: ___________________________________________________ Interviewer: ________________________________________________ Date: ______________________________________________________ I agree to participate in an interview for [project/publication/podcast/research]. I understand: • The interview will be used for: [describe purpose] • My participation is voluntary — I can stop at any time • I may skip any question without explanation • My responses will be [kept private / attributed to me by name] RECORDING: ☐ I consent to this interview being audio recorded. ☐ I consent to this interview being video recorded. ☐ I do NOT consent to being recorded. IDENTIFICATION: ☐ My name may be used. ☐ Please keep me anonymous. Participant Name: ________________________________ Signature: _______________________________________ Date: ____________ Interviewer Signature: ____________________________ Date: ____________

Basic Consent Form for Interview (Research)

For basic research interviews that still need to meet minimum ethical standards without the full complexity of IRB-required forms, use this slightly expanded simple template. It covers the key elements while remaining readable and non-intimidating for participants.

BASIC RESEARCH INTERVIEW CONSENT FORM Study: ___________________________________________ Date: ___________ Researcher: _____________________________________ Institution: ___________ I agree to participate in a research interview for the above study. I understand: • Purpose: This interview will be used to [describe use]. • Duration: The interview will take approximately [X] minutes. • Recording: ☐ Audio recorded ☐ Video recorded ☐ No recording • Confidentiality: My responses will be [anonymized / used with my name]. • I can stop at any time with no consequences. • Contact for questions: [email/phone] Participant: _______________________ Signature: ______________________ Date: ________ Researcher: _______________________ Signature: ______________________ Date: ________

Oral Interview Consent Form

Oral history interviews — for school projects, family histories, community archives, or historical documentation — benefit from a simple, accessible consent form. The key elements are the same: purpose, recording consent, and voluntary participation. For oral history archive projects, also consider adding a long-term preservation statement allowing future access to the recording.

When to Use a More Comprehensive Form

Important: A simple consent form is NOT appropriate for formal academic research requiring IRB approval, research involving vulnerable populations (minors, patients, prisoners), research with more than minimal risk, or research that may be published in peer-reviewed journals. For these, use a full informed consent form →

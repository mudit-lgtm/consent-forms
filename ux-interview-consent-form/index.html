What to Include

  • Company/team name and researcher contact details
  • Purpose: describe product, service, or design being studied
  • What the session involves (interview, task completion, think-aloud)
  • Recording: audio, video, screen recording options
  • Observer consent (if other team members are watching)
  • Internal use only vs. presentation/publication rights
  • GDPR and data privacy compliance details
  • Right to decline tasks or stop the session at any time
  • Incentive or compensation information
  • Note-taking and quote-use permissions
  • Data retention and deletion schedule
  • Signature lines for participant and researcher

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a UX interview consent form? ▼
A UX interview consent form is signed by research participants before user research sessions, including user interviews, usability tests, contextual inquiries, and participatory design activities. It covers recording rights, data use, and GDPR compliance.
Does UX research require a consent form? ▼
Yes. Any user research session where participants are being recorded, observed, or where data is being collected for design decisions should have documented consent. This is both an ethical requirement and best practice.
Do I need GDPR consent for UX research? ▼
If you are collecting data from EU residents, GDPR requires a clear legal basis for data processing. Consent is one valid basis. Your consent form should specify what data is collected, how it's used, the retention period, and participants' rights.
What is a 'think-aloud' session in UX research? ▼
A think-aloud session is a usability research method where participants verbalize their thoughts as they complete tasks with a product or prototype. Consent to record and use these verbalizations should be covered in the consent form.
Should I include a screen recording consent option? ▼
Yes, especially for remote UX research. Some participants may be comfortable with audio recording but not screen recording, or vice versa. Offering separate checkboxes for each type of recording respects participant autonomy.

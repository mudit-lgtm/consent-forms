How to Use This Interview Consent Form Template

  1. Download the Template

    Choose PDF (for printing and signing) or Word (.docx) for full editing capability.

  2. Customize the Template

    Replace all bracketed placeholders with your study title, researcher name, institution, and specific consent language.

  3. Review with Your Ethics Committee

    If your research requires IRB approval, submit the draft consent form as part of your ethics application.

  4. Share with Participants

    Provide the form to participants before the interview by email, in person, or via a digital signing platform.

  5. Collect Signed Copies

    Retain a signed copy for your records and provide a copy to the participant. Store securely per your data management plan.

What to Include in Your Interview Consent Form Template

  • Study/project title and researcher names
  • Name of institution or organization sponsoring the research
  • Clear statement of the study’s purpose
  • What the participant will be asked to do and for how long
  • Whether sessions will be recorded (audio/video)
  • How recordings and data will be stored and used
  • Anonymity, pseudonymity, or identified participation options
  • Statement of voluntary participation and right to withdraw
  • Contact information for the researcher and ethics committee
  • Signature and date fields for participant and researcher

Interview Consent Form Template Sample

INTERVIEW CONSENT FORM

Study Title: [Insert Study Title]
Researcher: [Researcher Name], [Institution]
Contact: [Email Address] | [Phone Number]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PURPOSE
You are invited to participate in a research interview. The study aims to [describe
research objective]. This project has been approved by [IRB/Ethics Board Name],
Protocol No. [XXXX].

WHAT YOU WILL BE ASKED TO DO
You will be interviewed for approximately [duration]. The interview will cover topics
related to [subject matter]. With your permission, the interview will be [audio/video]
recorded for transcription purposes only.

YOUR RIGHTS
• Participation is entirely voluntary
• You may withdraw at any time without penalty
• You may decline to answer any question
• You may request that recording be stopped at any time

CONFIDENTIALITY
All information you provide will be kept confidential. [Describe storage method,
anonymization approach, and data retention period.]

RISKS AND BENEFITS
The risks of participation are minimal. There are no direct benefits to you, though your
participation will contribute to [research purpose].

CONSENT

I have read and understood the information above. I voluntarily agree to participate.

Participant Name (print): ______________________________

Participant Signature: _______________________________ Date: __________

Researcher Signature: ________________________________ Date: __________

Interview Consent Form Template FAQs

What is an interview consent form template? ▼
An interview consent form template is a pre-formatted document with placeholder fields that you fill in with your specific study details. It saves time and ensures you include all legally and ethically required elements.
Is the Word template fully editable? ▼
Yes. The Word (.docx) template can be edited in Microsoft Word, Google Docs, or any compatible application. You can customize fonts, add your institution logo, and modify the consent language as needed.
Can I use this template for IRB submissions? ▼
Yes. This template covers the standard elements required by most IRBs. However, you should review your specific institution's IRB requirements and modify the template accordingly before submission.
Do I need a separate template for different interview types? ▼
This general template works for most scenarios. For specialized interviews (IRB, journalism, podcast, UX research), we provide specific templates tailored to those contexts.
How should I store signed consent forms? ▼
Store signed consent forms securely in accordance with your institution's data retention policy. For digital forms, use encrypted cloud storage. Typically, forms should be retained for a minimum of 3–5 years after study completion.

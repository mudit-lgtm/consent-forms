Why You Need a Recording Consent Form

Recording an interview without explicit consent may violate federal and state wiretapping laws, privacy statutes, and research ethics requirements. The U.S. federal "one-party consent" law allows recording if one party (the interviewer) consents, but many states require all-party (two-party) consent — meaning you must obtain the interviewee's explicit permission before recording.

Beyond legal requirements, recording consent forms are required by IRBs for research, journalism codes of ethics, and podcast industry standards. A written recording consent form protects both parties.

Legal Note: Recording consent laws vary by state and country. California, Florida, Pennsylvania, and many other states are "all-party consent" states. Always obtain written recording consent before recording any interview. This is not legal advice — consult an attorney for specific guidance.

Audio Recording Consent Form Template

AUDIO RECORDING CONSENT FORM Project/Study Title: _________________________________________ Interviewer/Researcher: ______________________________________ Date: _______________________________________________________ CONSENT TO AUDIO RECORD I, [Participant Name], consent to being audio recorded during my interview with [Interviewer Name] on [Date]. I understand that: • The audio recording will be used for: [transcription / research analysis / podcast / broadcast / publication] • The recording will be [stored securely / deleted after transcription / archived for X years] • The recording [will / will not] be shared with third parties • My name [will / will not] be associated with the recording in any publication I [do / do not] consent to the audio recording being used in final published/broadcast materials. Participant Name (Print): ____________________________________ Participant Signature: _______________________________________ Date: ___________ Interviewer Signature: _______________________________________ Date: ___________

Video Recording Consent Form Template

Video recording consent requires additional considerations around image rights, distribution, and whether the participant may be identifiable in the final content.

VIDEO RECORDING CONSENT FORM Project/Study Title: _________________________________________ Interviewer/Organization: ____________________________________ Date of Recording: ___________________________________________ CONSENT TO VIDEO RECORD I, [Participant Name], grant permission to [Interviewer/Organization] to video record my interview on [Date]. PERMITTED USES (check all that apply): ☐ Internal research/transcription only ☐ Educational or academic publication ☐ Online distribution (YouTube, social media, website) ☐ Broadcast (TV, documentary, news) ☐ Podcast or audio-only use of the video recording IMAGE RIGHTS: ☐ I consent to my face being visible in published/broadcast materials. ☐ I prefer my face to be blurred or obscured. ☐ I prefer audio-only use (voice without visual image). DURATION: This consent applies for [ ] years from the date of recording, OR [ ] indefinitely. I understand I may withdraw this consent within [X days] of the recording date. Participant Name (Print): ____________________________________ Participant Signature: _______________________________________ Date: ___________ Interviewer/Producer Signature: ______________________________ Date: ___________

Verbal vs. Written Recording Consent

Written consent is always preferable and often legally required. A signed recording consent form creates a documented record of permission. For most research, journalism, and media contexts, always use written consent.

Verbal consent may be acceptable in some limited contexts (e.g., phone interviews, live radio). If recording verbal consent, best practice is to begin the recording with a spoken consent statement: "I am recording this interview. Do you consent to being recorded? [Participant says 'Yes.']" Keep the recording as documentation.

Generic Recording Interview Consent Form

Need a simple, all-purpose recording consent form? This generic template works for most contexts:

RECORDING CONSENT FORM I consent to [Interviewer/Organization] recording my interview on [Date]. I understand the recording will be used for [purpose]. ☐ Audio recording ☐ Video recording ☐ Both Participant: ________________________ Signature: ________________________ Date: ________ Interviewer: ________________________ Signature: ________________________ Date: ________

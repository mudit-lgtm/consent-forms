Why Research Interviews Require Consent Forms

Any research study involving interviews with human subjects must obtain informed consent before data collection begins. This requirement stems from foundational documents in research ethics, including the Belmont Report and the Declaration of Helsinki, and is enforced by Institutional Review Boards (IRBs) at virtually every university and research institution.

Research interview consent forms protect participants by ensuring they understand what they are agreeing to — including how their responses will be used, whether their identity will be protected, and that they may withdraw at any time. They also protect researchers by demonstrating ethical compliance.

IRB Requirements for Research Interview Consent

IRB requirements for research interview consent forms typically mandate:

  • Statement of purpose and study procedures in plain language
  • Expected duration of the participant's involvement
  • Description of any foreseeable risks (even if minimal)
  • Statement of any benefits to participant or society
  • Confidentiality and data protection provisions
  • Statement that participation is voluntary and withdrawal is penalty-free
  • Contact information for the researcher and IRB office
  • IRB protocol approval number and approval date

Tip: Your IRB will review your consent form before approving your study. Use plain language (6th–8th grade reading level), avoid passive voice, and don't use technical jargon. See full IRB consent form guidance →

Sample Research Interview Consent Form

The following is a full, ready-to-use research interview consent form template meeting standard IRB requirements:

RESEARCH INTERVIEW CONSENT FORM Study Title: [Full Research Study Title] Principal Investigator: [Name], [Department], [University/Institution] Co-Investigators: [Names, if applicable] IRB Protocol Number: [#] IRB Approval Date: [Date] Funding Source: [If applicable] Contact Email: [Email] Phone: [Phone] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ PURPOSE OF THE RESEARCH You are being invited to participate in a research study about [topic]. The purpose of this study is to [describe aims]. This research is significant because [explain importance]. PROCEDURES If you agree to participate, you will take part in a [type: structured/semi-structured] interview lasting approximately [X] minutes. The interview will be conducted [location/platform]. You will be asked about your experiences with and perspectives on [topics]. With your permission, the interview will be audio-recorded and transcribed. You may review the transcript of your interview upon request. RISKS Participation involves minimal risk. The main foreseeable risks are [discomfort discussing sensitive topics / time commitment]. You may decline to answer any question without penalty. BENEFITS There is no direct compensation or personal benefit for participating. Your responses will contribute to [describe academic or societal benefit]. CONFIDENTIALITY Your identity will be protected. Your name will be replaced with a code or pseudonym in all research materials. Interview recordings will be stored in password-protected files accessible only to the research team. Recordings will be destroyed after transcription. Anonymized transcripts will be retained for [X years] for research verification. VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION Participation is completely voluntary. You may refuse to participate or withdraw from the study at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. CONTACT INFORMATION Questions about this research: [PI Name], [email], [phone] Questions about your rights as a participant: [IRB Office], [email], [phone] ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ CONSENT I have read the information above. My questions have been answered. I voluntarily agree to participate in this research study. Participant Name (Print): ____________________________ Participant Signature: _______________________________ Date: ______________________________________________ Researcher Name (Print): ____________________________ Researcher Signature: ________________________________ Date: ______________________________________________

Consent Form for Interview Research in Social Science & Anthropology

Social science and anthropology research interviews often involve sensitive cultural, political, or personal topics. These consent forms typically emphasize confidentiality, voluntary participation, and the right to review transcripts. For ethnographic interviews, see qualitative interview consent forms.

Anthropology research consent forms may also need to address:

  • Community or tribal consent (in addition to individual consent)
  • Cultural sensitivities around recording and data use
  • Language and translation considerations for non-English speakers
  • Long-term data archiving policies for oral history projects

Psychology Research Interview Consent Form

Psychology research interviews often address sensitive mental health, behavioral, or personal history topics. Consent forms for psychology research must be especially clear about:

  • Potential for emotional distress and available support resources
  • Limits of confidentiality (e.g., mandatory reporting for harm-to-self)
  • Whether a debrief will occur after the interview
  • APA ethical guidelines compliance statement

Research Consent Form to Record Interviews

If your research interviews will be audio or video recorded, you must obtain explicit consent for recording separately. Many consent forms use checkboxes to allow participants to consent to the interview but not to recording. View recording consent form templates →

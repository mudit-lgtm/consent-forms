Media Interview Consent Form

Free media interview consent forms for press, TV, documentary, and street interviews. Covers recording, broadcast, and publication rights.

Media Interview Consent: What You Need to Know

Media interview consent forms are used by journalists, documentary filmmakers, TV producers, news organizations, and social media creators to obtain permission to record, broadcast, and publish interviews. Unlike research consent forms, media consent forms focus primarily on intellectual property rights, recording permissions, and publication/broadcast rights.

Different media contexts require different consent considerations:

  • TV/Broadcast — Must cover broadcast rights, re-runs, streaming, and international distribution
  • Documentary Film — Must address film festival screening, theatrical release, and streaming rights
  • Online/Social Media — Must cover specific platform permissions (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, etc.)
  • Street Interviews — Verbal or written consent for brief public interviews
  • Print/News — On-the-record, off-the-record, and quote review provisions

Media Interview Consent Form Template

MEDIA INTERVIEW CONSENT AND RELEASE FORM Media Organization: _________________________________________ Producer/Journalist: _________________________________________ Production Title/Story: ________________________________________ Date of Interview: ____________________________________________ Interview Location: ___________________________________________ ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ CONSENT TO INTERVIEW, RECORD, AND PUBLISH I, [Participant/Interviewee Name], consent to being interviewed by [Journalist/Producer Name] of [Media Organization] in connection with [Production Title/Story]. RECORDING CONSENT: ☐ Audio recording ☐ Video recording ☐ Photography ☐ I do NOT consent to recording (note-taking only) PUBLICATION AND DISTRIBUTION RIGHTS: I grant [Media Organization] the right to use my name, image, voice, and statements in: ☐ Print publication (newspaper, magazine, book) ☐ Online publication (website, digital article) ☐ Broadcast television ☐ Streaming platforms (Netflix, YouTube, etc.) ☐ Documentary film ☐ Social media ☐ All of the above QUOTE REVIEW: ☐ I understand my quotes may be used without review. ☐ I request the opportunity to review direct quotes before publication. INTERVIEW STATUS: ☐ ON THE RECORD — Name and statements may be used and attributed directly. ☐ ON BACKGROUND — Information may be used without attribution to me. ☐ OFF THE RECORD — Information shared will not be published. STATEMENT STATUS: I understand that any statements made on the record may be published verbatim or paraphrased. VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION: My participation is voluntary. I may withdraw before publication without penalty. ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ Participant Name (Print): _____________________________________ Participant Signature: ________________________________________ Date: ___________ Journalist/Producer Signature: _________________________________ Date: ___________

Street Interview Consent Form

For quick street interviews (vox pops), a simplified verbal or short written consent form is typically used. The key elements are: permission to record, permission to broadcast/publish, and whether the interviewee's name will be used. Always carry printed consent forms when filming in public, especially for broadcast-quality content.

Consent Form for Documentary Interviews

Documentary interviews often involve extended subjects who appear throughout a film. Documentary consent forms should address: all distribution formats (theatrical, streaming, DVD, TV), international rights, duration of license (typically "in perpetuity"), and whether participants will receive any payment or screen credit. See video interview consent forms →

Artist Interview Consent Form

Artists, musicians, and public figures being interviewed about their work may require specialized consent forms that address intellectual property rights, the use of their artwork or performances in the media piece, and commercial use provisions. Always consult a media lawyer for high-stakes commercial productions.

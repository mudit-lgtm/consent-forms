What Is an IRB and Why Does It Require Consent Forms?

An Institutional Review Board (IRB) is a committee established to review and approve research involving human subjects. All U.S. universities and research institutions that receive federal funding are required to have an IRB under the Common Rule (45 CFR 46). The IRB's primary role is to protect the rights and welfare of research participants.

Before any research involving human subjects begins — including qualitative interviews — the researcher must submit their study for IRB review. As part of this process, the IRB reviews the informed consent form to ensure it meets regulatory requirements and adequately protects participant rights.

Key Point: You cannot conduct human subjects research interviews without IRB approval at an accredited institution. The consent form is a core part of IRB review. Obtain IRB approval before conducting any interviews.

IRB Requirements for Interview Consent Forms

Under 45 CFR 46.116, IRB-approved consent forms must include these required elements:

  • Statement that the study involves research, explanation of purposes, and expected duration of subject participation
  • Description of the procedures to be followed, including which are experimental
  • Description of any reasonably foreseeable risks or discomforts
  • Description of any reasonably expected benefits to the subject or others
  • Disclosure of appropriate alternative procedures or courses of treatment
  • Statement describing the extent of confidentiality and who may see the data
  • Explanation of compensation or treatment for research-related injuries (if more than minimal risk)
  • Contact information for questions about the research and participant rights
  • Statement that participation is voluntary, refusal involves no penalty, and subject may discontinue at any time

University-Specific IRB Consent Forms

While all IRB consent forms must meet federal requirements, individual universities often have additional requirements or preferred formats:

SNHU Interview Consent Form

Southern New Hampshire University requires IRB-approved consent forms for all research involving human subjects. SNHU's IRB follows APA and federal guidelines. Students must submit consent forms with their IRB application.

UCL Interview Consent Form

University College London follows UK Research Integrity Office guidelines and GDPR requirements. UCL consent forms must include a data processing statement under GDPR Article 13.

University of Edinburgh Consent Form

The University of Edinburgh's Research Ethics Committee requires informed consent forms meeting UK Research Integrity Office standards. Forms must include data protection and GDPR compliance statements.

Penn State IRB Consent Form

Penn State's IRB requires consent forms to follow their specific template, which includes a HIPAA authorization section for health-related research. Non-health research follows the standard 45 CFR 46 format.

Important: Always check your specific university's IRB website for their current required consent form template. Requirements change and institutions may have their own formatting rules.

IRB Written Consent for Interview Form

IRBs distinguish between full written consent and waived consent. A waiver of written consent (per 45 CFR 46.117(c)) may be granted when: the research presents no more than minimal risk, involves no procedures for which written consent is normally required outside research, and the waiver will not adversely affect participant rights. For most interview research, written signed consent is required.

Verbal consent scripts (used instead of written forms) must also be IRB-approved. They require the same informational elements as written consent forms.

UX Interview Consent Form

Free UX interview consent form for user research. Covers screen recording, session data, GDPR compliance, and confidentiality for user interviews and usability testing.

Why UX Researchers Need Consent Forms

User Experience (UX) researchers conduct interviews, usability tests, and user research sessions to understand how people interact with products and services. These sessions often involve recording screens, audio, and video — and collecting behavioral data that must be handled with care.

A UX interview consent form ensures participants understand:

  • What the session involves and how long it will take
  • What will be recorded (screen, audio, video, eye tracking, etc.)
  • How their data will be used (internal research, product improvement, published insights)
  • Whether they will be identified in any reports or presentations
  • Their right to stop the session at any time
  • How long their data will be retained

What to Include in a UX Interview Consent Form

  • Session purpose and duration
  • Recording permissions (screen, audio, video, eye tracking)
  • Data use statement (internal research / publications / presentations)
  • Confidentiality and anonymization provisions
  • GDPR or CCPA compliance statement (if applicable)
  • Voluntary participation and right to withdraw
  • Incentive or compensation details (if applicable)
  • Researcher and participant signature block

Consent for Recording UX Sessions

UX session recording consent is particularly important because sessions may capture sensitive user behavior, private accounts, personal data on screen, and facial expressions. Always obtain explicit, itemized consent for each type of recording (screen, audio, video, eye tracking) separately. Participants should be able to consent to some recording types but not others.

GDPR Compliance for UX Research Consent

If you conduct UX research with participants in the EU/EEA, your consent form must comply with GDPR. This means:

  • Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
  • You must clearly state the legal basis for data processing (Article 6)
  • Participants must be informed of their rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability)
  • You must name a Data Protection Officer or privacy contact
  • Withdrawal of consent must be as easy as giving it

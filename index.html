Definition

What Is an Interview Consent Form?

An interview consent form is a document that a participant signs before taking part in an interview. It formally grants the researcher, journalist, or interviewer permission to conduct, record, and use the interview content for a specified purpose. Consent forms protect both the interviewer and the interviewee by establishing clear expectations about how the interview will be conducted and how the data will be used.

Interview consent forms are used across a wide range of fields including academic research, journalism, UX design, podcasting, documentary filmmaking, and corporate research. Depending on the context, they may be required by an Institutional Review Board (IRB), a university ethics committee, a media organization's legal team, or simply recommended as best practice.

A well-written interview consent form covers the study purpose, what participants will be asked to do, whether the interview will be recorded, how data will be stored and protected, the participant's right to withdraw at any time, and contact information for questions or concerns.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About Interview Consent Forms

What is an interview consent form? ▼
An interview consent form is a document signed by a participant before an interview that grants the researcher or interviewer permission to record, use, or publish the conversation. It protects both parties and ensures voluntary, informed participation.
Are interview consent forms legally required? ▼
For academic research, most Institutional Review Boards (IRBs) require signed informed consent before interviewing human subjects. For journalism, podcasts, and media, consent forms are strongly recommended best practice and may be legally required in your jurisdiction, particularly in two-party consent states or countries with strict privacy laws.
What should an interview consent form include? ▼
An interview consent form should include: the study title, researcher name and institution, purpose of the study, expected interview duration, whether the interview will be recorded, how data will be stored and protected, the participant's right to withdraw at any time, confidentiality and anonymity provisions, and signature lines for both the participant and researcher.
Can I fill out an interview consent form online? ▼
Yes. InterviewConsentForms lets you fill in all required fields online using the interactive form tool on each page. You can then download the completed form as a PDF for printing or as a Word document for further editing — completely free, with no account required.
Do I need consent forms for telephone or online interviews? ▼
While written consent forms are not always legally required for telephone interviews, they are strongly recommended for research purposes. For online (Zoom, Skype, video) interviews, a written or electronic consent form is best practice and may be required by your IRB or ethics committee. An oral consent statement recorded at the start of a call can serve as an alternative in some cases.

What Is an Interview Consent Form?

An interview consent form is a written agreement signed by a participant before taking part in an interview. It documents that the interviewee has been fully informed about the purpose of the interview, how their responses will be used, whether the interview will be recorded, and their rights as a participant — including the right to withdraw at any time without penalty.

Interview consent forms are used in academic research, journalism, UX research, podcasting, documentary filmmaking, and any other context where an interview is conducted and the content may be recorded, published, or shared.

What to Include in an Interview Consent Form

  • Study title, researcher name, and institutional affiliation
  • Purpose and description of the interview
  • Expected duration of participation
  • Whether the interview will be recorded (audio/video)
  • How data will be stored, used, and protected
  • Participant’s right to withdraw at any time
  • Confidentiality and anonymity provisions
  • Contact information for questions or concerns
  • Signature lines for participant and researcher

Study Title: [Insert Study Title]
Researcher: [Researcher Name], [Institution]
Contact: [Email Address] | [Phone Number]

PURPOSE
You are invited to participate in a research interview. The study aims to [describe
research objective]. This project has been approved by [IRB/Ethics Board Name],
Protocol No. [XXXX].

WHAT YOU WILL BE ASKED TO DO
You will be interviewed for approximately [duration]. The interview will cover topics
related to [subject matter]. With your permission, the interview will be [audio/video]
recorded for transcription purposes only.

YOUR RIGHTS
• Participation is entirely voluntary
• You may withdraw at any time without penalty
• You may decline to answer any question
• You may request that recording be stopped at any time

CONFIDENTIALITY
All information you provide will be kept confidential. [Describe storage method,
anonymization approach, and data retention period.]

RISKS AND BENEFITS
The risks of participation are minimal. There are no direct benefits to you, though your
participation will contribute to [research purpose].

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of an interview consent form? ▼
The purpose of an interview consent form is to ensure that participants are fully informed about the nature of the interview, how their data will be used, and their rights before agreeing to take part. It protects both the researcher and the participant.
Is an interview consent form legally binding? ▼
A properly completed interview consent form creates a documented record of informed voluntary consent, which can have legal weight. However, its enforceability varies by jurisdiction and context. It is not a contract but evidence of informed consent.
Can I use this interview consent form for any type of interview? ▼
Yes. This general interview consent form template covers the core elements required for most interview types. For specialized contexts such as IRB research, journalism, or UX research, we recommend using the specific template for your use case.
Do participants need to sign the consent form before the interview? ▼
Yes. The consent form should always be signed before the interview begins. For online interviews, this can be done via a digital signature or email confirmation.
What if a participant changes their mind after signing? ▼
Participants retain the right to withdraw their consent at any time. If a participant withdraws after signing, you should stop collecting data from them and, if requested, remove any data already collected.
Can I email a consent form to participants? ▼
Yes. You can email the consent form as a PDF or Word document for the participant to sign and return. Electronic signatures are generally acceptable for research and media interviews.

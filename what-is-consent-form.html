What Is an Interview Consent Form?

A complete guide to understanding interview consent forms — their definition, purpose, legal requirements, and when they are and aren't required.

Definition: Interview Consent Form

An interview consent form is a legal document signed by a participant (the person being interviewed) before an interview takes place. It grants the interviewer — whether a researcher, journalist, podcaster, documentary filmmaker, or UX researcher — explicit permission to conduct the interview and to use the participant's responses in the manner described in the form.

The form ensures that consent is informed: the participant understands what they are agreeing to before signing. This distinguishes a consent form from a simple verbal agreement, which may not be sufficient for ethical, legal, or institutional purposes.

Key Term: "Informed consent" means the participant has received all relevant information about the interview — its purpose, how their data will be used, risks, and their rights — before agreeing to participate. Simply saying "is it OK if I record this?" does not constitute informed consent.

Purpose of an Interview Consent Form

Interview consent forms serve multiple purposes for both participants and interviewers:

For Participants

  • Ensures they are fully informed before agreeing
  • Documents their rights (to withdraw, remain anonymous, etc.)
  • Provides a record of what they consented to
  • Protects them from unauthorized use of their responses

For Interviewers/Researchers

  • Documents ethical compliance for IRB/institutional review
  • Provides legal protection if consent is later disputed
  • Establishes clear terms for data use and publication
  • Enables publication and broadcast of interview content

When Is a Consent Form Required?

Interview consent forms are required in the following contexts:

  • Academic/scientific research — IRB approval mandates informed consent for all human subjects research interviews
  • University research projects — including dissertations, theses, and course-based research
  • Recorded broadcast/podcast interviews — legal best practice and often required by platforms
  • Documentary and film interviews — required for distribution and broadcast licensing
  • UX research with session recording — required under privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA) and professional standards
  • Journalism involving vulnerable populations — children, crime victims, medical patients, etc.
  • Any interview where recording will occur — many states require all-party consent for recording

Are Consent Forms Required for Telephone Interviews?

Written consent forms are generally NOT required for casual telephone conversations, but they ARE required for:

  • IRB-approved research conducted by telephone
  • Recorded telephone interviews (varies by state — many require all-party consent)
  • Telephone interviews as part of a clinical or health research study
  • Systematic research data collection by telephone (surveys, structured interviews)

The principle that "written consent forms usually are not required for telephone interviews" applies primarily to journalistic contexts where the interviewer is having a casual, unrecorded conversation. In research contexts, IRBs typically require consent even for telephone interviews — though they may grant a waiver allowing verbal consent in lieu of written forms for minimal-risk studies.

Why Would an Employer Send a Consent Form Before a Second Interview?

If you received a consent form from an employer before a second job interview, this is almost certainly a background check consent form — not a research or interview consent form. This is a completely different type of document.

Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), employers in the United States must obtain written consent before conducting a background check on a job applicant. This background check consent form grants the employer permission to:

  • Check criminal history
  • Verify employment history and references
  • Check credit history (if relevant to the position)
  • Verify educational credentials
  • Conduct drug testing

Sending this form before a second interview is standard practice — it means the employer is seriously considering you and is preparing for next steps. It is not a red flag. Sign and return it promptly.

Note: The background check consent form provided by an employer before a job interview is different from the interview consent forms provided on this site, which are for research, journalism, podcasts, and similar interview contexts.

Interview Consent Form vs. Release Form — What's the Difference?

Document What It Covers Typical Context
Interview Consent Form Permission to conduct and record the interview; participant rights and data use Academic research, journalism, podcasts
Media Release Form Permission to publish, broadcast, or distribute the recorded content TV, documentary, social media, news
Background Check Consent Permission to conduct an employment background check Job applications, hiring process
Informed Consent Form Full research ethics consent meeting IRB standards; includes risks, benefits, and all participant rights Academic/scientific research, clinical studies

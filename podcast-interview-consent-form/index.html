What to Include

  • Podcast name, host name, and production company
  • Guest name and contact information
  • Recording consent: audio and/or video
  • Publishing rights: streaming platforms, YouTube, social media
  • Editing consent: right to edit content for time and clarity
  • Syndication and repurposing rights (clips, transcripts, newsletters)
  • Episode title or topic
  • Expected publication date and platform(s)
  • Right of guest to review episode before publication (if offered)
  • Any restrictions on content or sensitive topics
  • Compensation or promotional arrangement (if any)
  • Signatures of host and guest

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do podcast hosts need a consent form for every guest? ▼
Yes, it is strongly recommended. A podcast consent form documents that guests agreed to be recorded, published, and potentially repurposed across platforms — protecting you from future disputes.
What is a podcast guest release form? ▼
A podcast guest release form (also called a podcast consent form) grants the host the right to record, publish, edit, and distribute the guest's interview. It is similar to a media release form.
Does my podcast consent form need to cover YouTube and social media? ▼
Yes. If you publish video versions or clips on YouTube, Instagram, or other platforms, your consent form should explicitly cover all channels where the content may appear.
What if a guest changes their mind after recording? ▼
Include a clause in your consent form specifying the timeframe within which a guest can request removal before publication. Once published, removal is at your discretion unless legally required.
Do I need a consent form for documentary interviews? ▼
Yes. Documentary interview consent forms typically cover similar elements to podcast forms — recording rights, publication rights, editing consent, and attribution — but may also cover film festival screenings, broadcast, and commercial distribution.

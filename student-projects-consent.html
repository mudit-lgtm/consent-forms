Interview Consent Form for Student Projects

Free interview consent forms for student projects, AP Research, school assignments, and history papers. Includes parent consent for interviewing minors.

Why Student Research Projects Need Consent Forms

Student researchers — from high school AP Research students to college undergraduates — must follow the same ethical principles as professional researchers when conducting interviews for projects, papers, or capstone studies. A properly completed interview consent form protects both the student and the participant.

AP Research Interview Consent Requirements

College Board's AP Research course requires students to obtain IRB-like ethical approval and signed informed consent from interview participants. The AP Research Academic Paper must include documentation of ethical research practices. Student consent forms for AP Research should include:

  • Description of the AP Research study purpose
  • Participant's rights including voluntary participation and withdrawal
  • Confidentiality provisions
  • Recording consent (if applicable)
  • Contact information for the supervising teacher
  • Signature of participant (and parent/guardian if minor)

Parent Consent Form for Interview (Minor Participants)

If your research involves interviewing participants under 18 years old, you MUST obtain consent from a parent or legal guardian in addition to the minor's assent. The parent consent form must explain the same information as the participant form. Minor participants should also sign an assent form appropriate to their age and comprehension level.

Oral Interview Consent Form for Student Projects

For student history and oral history projects, an oral interview consent form documents that a historical subject has agreed to be interviewed and for their account to be recorded and potentially archived. These forms often have long-term archiving implications and should address future use of the recording.

Interview Consent for Sociology & History School Projects

High school and college sociology and history projects frequently involve community interviews. Students should adapt the template above for their specific subject matter, adding relevant details about how the material will be used (class paper, school archive, public presentation, etc.).

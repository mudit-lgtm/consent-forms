Journalism Interview Consent Form

Journalism interview consent form templates for reporters and students. Covers on-the-record, off-the-record, and recording consent for print and broadcast journalism.

Do Journalists Need Consent Forms?

Journalism ethics and legal best practices strongly recommend obtaining explicit consent — ideally in writing — before recording an interview or quoting a source by name. While journalists in many U.S. states can legally record one-party-consent conversations, professional standards and many newsrooms require documented consent, especially for audio/video recording.

Consent forms are particularly important for:

  • Recorded broadcast or podcast interviews
  • Vulnerable or anonymous sources
  • Documentary journalism projects
  • Student journalism assignments
  • Sensitive topics where a source might later dispute quotes

On-the-Record Interview Consent Form

JOURNALISM INTERVIEW CONSENT FORM Publication/Media Organization: ______________________________ Journalist: _________________________________________________ Story/Assignment: ____________________________________________ Date: ______________________________________________________ I, [Interviewee Name], agree to be interviewed by [Journalist Name] for [Publication/Organization]. ON-THE-RECORD STATUS: ☐ ON THE RECORD — I consent to my name, title, and statements being quoted directly in published content. ☐ ON BACKGROUND — My information may be reported but without identifying me by name. ☐ DEEP BACKGROUND — Information shared may guide reporting but will not be reported or attributed. ☐ OFF THE RECORD — Statements shared will not be reported in any form. RECORDING CONSENT: ☐ I consent to this interview being audio recorded. ☐ I consent to this interview being video recorded. ☐ I do NOT consent to recording. PUBLICATION RIGHTS: ☐ I consent to being quoted in print/online articles. ☐ I consent to being included in broadcast/podcast content. ☐ I would like the opportunity to review direct quotes for accuracy before publication. VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION: My participation is voluntary. I may decline to answer any question or withdraw from the interview at any time. Interviewee Name (Print): ___________________________________ Interviewee Signature: ______________________________________ Date: __________ Journalist Signature: ________________________________________ Date: __________

Student Journalism Interview Consent Form

Student journalists — whether working for campus newspapers, journalism class assignments, or multimedia projects — should always obtain written consent before recording or quoting sources. Student journalism programs at universities like SNHU require students to demonstrate ethical journalism practices including proper consent documentation.

SNHU Journalism Interview Consent Form

Southern New Hampshire University journalism students are required to obtain interview consent forms for interviews conducted as part of academic assignments. The consent form must document the interviewee's agreement to be quoted, recorded, and/or identified. The template above meets SNHU journalism program requirements.

What to Include

  • Student researcher name, school/class, and supervising teacher
  • Project or assignment title and purpose
  • Simple, plain-language description of the interview
  • Expected duration and topics to be covered
  • Whether the interview will be recorded
  • How the interview data will be used (class presentation, report, etc.)
  • Participant's right to decline or stop at any time
  • For minors: parent/guardian signature line in addition to student signature
  • Contact information for student and supervising teacher
  • Oral consent option for informal interviews

Student Researcher: [Student Name], [School Name]
Class / Project: [Course Name / AP Research / History Project]

WHAT WILL HAPPEN

• You do not have to answer any question you are not comfortable with
• You may stop the interview at any time

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do student researchers need a consent form for interviews? ▼
Yes, for any formal academic project including AP Research, oral history projects, and school-based research that involves interviewing people. Consent forms protect both you as the interviewer and your participants.
When do I need parent/guardian consent for student interviews? ▼
You need parent/guardian consent whenever you are interviewing someone under 18 years old. The parent or guardian must sign in addition to — not instead of — the minor participant's assent signature (if age-appropriate).
Can I get oral consent instead of a written signature? ▼
For informal student projects, oral consent at the start of a recording (stating who you are, the project purpose, and confirming the interviewee's willingness) may be acceptable. Check with your supervising teacher or class requirements.
What is an AP Research interview consent form? ▼
AP Research is an advanced placement course where students conduct original research. AP Research students typically need to complete an IRB-like academic research process, which includes obtaining signed consent forms from interview participants.
How do I write a simple consent form for a school history project? ▼
Keep it short and simple: state your name and school, describe the project in one sentence, explain what the interview involves, state how you'll protect their privacy, and include a signature line. See our simple interview consent form for a one-page example.

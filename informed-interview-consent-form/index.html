What to Include

  • Full description of the study purpose and research questions
  • Expected duration and nature of participation
  • Potential risks and discomforts to participants
  • Direct and indirect benefits of participation
  • Confidentiality and anonymity measures
  • Data storage, retention, and destruction policy
  • Statement that participation is voluntary
  • Right to withdraw at any time without penalty
  • IRB/ethics committee approval reference
  • Researcher and institutional contact information
  • Participant's right to ask questions at any time
  • Signature and date fields for participant and researcher

Study Title: [Study Title]

ABOUT THIS STUDY
You are invited to participate in a research study examining [topic]. This
research is being conducted by [researcher name] at [institution].

PURPOSE
The purpose of this study is to [describe objectives and significance].

YOUR PARTICIPATION
You are being asked to take part in a one-on-one interview lasting approximately
[duration]. You will be asked about [topic areas]. There are no right or wrong
answers — your personal perspective is what matters.

RISKS
Participation involves minimal risk. Some questions may touch on [sensitive
topic]. You may skip any question or stop the interview at any time.

BENEFITS
You will not directly benefit from participation. However, this research
contributes to [field/societal benefit].

CONFIDENTIALITY
Your responses will be kept strictly confidential. [Choose: Your name will not
be used / A pseudonym will be used / You will be identified with your consent.]
Data will be stored on an encrypted device and deleted after [period].

VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION
Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may decline to participate or
withdraw at any point, including after the interview, without penalty.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a consent form 'informed'? ▼
An 'informed' consent form means the participant has been given all the information they need to make a voluntary, knowledgeable decision about participation — including the study purpose, what they'll be asked to do, potential risks and benefits, and their rights.
Is informed consent required for all research interviews? ▼
Most institutional ethics boards and IRBs require informed consent for research involving human participants. The level of detail required varies by risk level and institution.
Can informed consent be given verbally? ▼
Yes, in some low-risk research settings, verbal consent may be acceptable. However, written informed consent is always preferred as it creates a documented record. Check your IRB's specific requirements.
What's the difference between informed consent and assent? ▼
Informed consent is given by adults who can legally agree to participate. Assent is a child's agreement to participate in addition to parental/guardian consent. Both may be required when interviewing minors.
How long should I keep signed consent forms? ▼
Most institutions require retention of consent forms for 3–7 years after study completion. Check your institution's records management policy.

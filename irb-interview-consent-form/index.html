What to Include

  • IRB protocol number and approval/expiry dates
  • IRB office contact information for participant questions
  • Principal investigator name, credentials, institution, and department
  • Clear and plain-language description of study purpose
  • Detailed description of interview procedures and duration
  • Statement of foreseeable risks and discomforts
  • Statement of benefits to participants or society
  • Confidentiality and data protection measures
  • Statement that participation is voluntary and withdrawal is penalty-free
  • Compensation information (if any)
  • Statement of any mandatory reporting obligations
  • Separate checkboxes for audio and video recording consent
  • Signature and date lines for participant and researcher

IRB INTERVIEW CONSENT FORM

Study Title: [Study Title]
Principal Investigator: [Name, Degree], [Department]
Institution: [University Name]
IRB Protocol Number: [XXXXXXXX]
Approval Date: [Date] | Expiry Date: [Date]
Contact: [Email] | [Phone]
IRB Office: [IRB Office Name], [Email], [Phone]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This research has been approved by the Institutional Review Board (IRB) of
[University Name]. If you have questions about your rights as a research
participant, contact the IRB at [IRB contact information].

PURPOSE OF RESEARCH
[1–2 paragraph description of research aims and significance]

PROCEDURES
You will participate in an interview lasting approximately [duration] covering
[topic areas]. The interview will be conducted [format] and [will/will not] be
recorded.

RISKS
[Describe specific risks. For most qualitative interview research: "Risks are
minimal and no greater than those encountered in everyday life."]

BENEFITS
[State direct and indirect benefits, or state "no direct benefits to participants."]

CONFIDENTIALITY
[Describe specific confidentiality measures, storage, access controls, retention,
and any limits to confidentiality (e.g., mandatory reporting obligations).]

VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION
Participation is voluntary. Refusal to participate will not result in any penalty
or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.

COMPENSATION
[State compensation offered, or "You will not receive compensation for this study."]

QUESTIONS
If you have questions about this research, contact [PI name] at [contact info].
For questions about your rights as a research participant, contact [IRB info].

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an IRB and what does it do? ▼
An Institutional Review Board (IRB) is a committee established to protect the rights and welfare of human research subjects. It reviews research protocols to ensure they meet ethical standards before data collection begins.
Is IRB approval required for interview research? ▼
IRB review is required for most research involving human subjects at universities and federally-funded institutions. Whether your specific interview study requires full review, expedited review, or qualifies for exemption depends on the level of risk and your institution.
What makes an IRB consent form different from a standard one? ▼
An IRB consent form must include specific elements required by federal regulations (45 CFR 46), including the IRB protocol number, IRB contact information, a statement on voluntary participation, and specific confidentiality provisions. Your institution's IRB office will have a checklist.
Does SNHU have a specific interview consent form? ▼
Yes, Southern New Hampshire University (SNHU) provides IRB-approved consent form templates for student research. Check SNHU's academic resources portal for the current template, which this form closely mirrors.
Can I use this form for UCL or University of Edinburgh research? ▼
This template covers internationally-recognized ethical research standards. However, UCL and the University of Edinburgh have their own ethics review processes and may require institution-specific language. Use this as a starting point and adapt it to your institution's requirements.

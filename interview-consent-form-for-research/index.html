What to Include

  • IRB protocol number and ethics approval details
  • Principal investigator name, credentials, and department
  • Clear statement of research purpose and objectives
  • Description of interview format and estimated duration
  • Topics to be covered in the interview
  • Audio/video recording permissions
  • Data storage, anonymization, and retention policy
  • Participant's right to withdraw without penalty
  • Confidentiality provisions and data access controls
  • Contact information for researcher and IRB
  • Statement of voluntary participation
  • Signature and date lines for participant and researcher

RESEARCH INTERVIEW CONSENT FORM

Study Title: [Full Study Title]
Principal Investigator: [Name, Degree]
Co-Investigators: [Names if applicable]
Department: [Department Name]
Institution: [University / Research Institute]
IRB Protocol No.: [XXXXXXXXXX]
Contact: [PI Email] | [Phone]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

INTRODUCTION
You are invited to participate in a research study. Please read this consent
form before deciding whether to participate. Your participation is voluntary.

BACKGROUND AND PURPOSE
This study examines [research topic]. The goal is to [research objective].
Findings will be used to [describe anticipated use/publication].

INTERVIEW PROCEDURE
If you agree to participate, you will be interviewed for approximately [duration].
The interview will take place [in person / online via Zoom / by telephone].
Topics will include [describe main topic areas].

RECORDING
With your permission, the interview will be audio recorded to ensure accurate
transcription. The recording will be used only for this research purpose and
will be destroyed after transcription is complete.

RISKS AND DISCOMFORTS
Risks are minimal. Some questions may address [sensitive topic]. You may
decline to answer any question without penalty.

BENEFITS
There are no direct benefits to you. Your participation will contribute to
[academic/social benefit].

CONFIDENTIALITY
All data will be treated as confidential. [Choose anonymization approach.]
Data will be stored on a password-protected system accessible only to the
research team. Published reports will not identify individual participants.

VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION AND WITHDRAWAL
Participation is voluntary. You may withdraw at any time before, during, or
after the interview without penalty or loss of benefits.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

When is a consent form required for a research interview? ▼
A consent form is required whenever you are interviewing human participants as part of formal research — particularly any research that requires IRB or ethics board approval. This includes academic, medical, social science, and corporate research.
What is IRB approval and do I need it? ▼
The Institutional Review Board (IRB) reviews research involving human subjects to ensure ethical standards are met. Most university and federally-funded research requires IRB approval before data collection begins.
Can I conduct research interviews without a consent form? ▼
For formal research, especially that requiring IRB approval, a signed consent form is typically mandatory. Some low-risk research may qualify for IRB exemption or expedited review, but consent documentation is still best practice.
What does 'participant confidentiality' mean? ▼
Participant confidentiality means the researcher keeps participants' identities and responses private — not sharing identifying information with anyone outside the research team and anonymizing published results.
How do I get IRB approval for my research interview study? ▼
Submit an IRB application through your institution's research compliance office. Include your research protocol, consent form, interview guide, and data management plan. Approval timelines vary from a few days to several months.
